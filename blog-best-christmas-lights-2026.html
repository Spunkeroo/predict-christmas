Best Christmas Lights 2026: LED, Smart & Outdoor Picks

Chicago goes all-out for Christmas — from the Magnificent Mile Lights Festival to Lincoln Park Zoo's ZooLights. Whether you're decorating a Wrigleyville bungalow, a Lincoln Park brownstone, or a suburban home in Naperville, the right lights make all the difference. Here are the best Christmas lights for 2026.

Best Smart Christmas Lights

Twinkly Strings ($80-$200) — The gold standard of smart Christmas lights. Each LED is individually addressable via the Twinkly app, letting you create millions of color combinations, animations, and effects. Map your lights in 3D and create custom displays synced to music. Works with Alexa and Google Home. Available in 100, 250, and 600 LED lengths. These are the same lights used in Chicago's Christkindlmarket decorations.

Govee RGBIC String Lights ($40-$80) — Best value smart option. WiFi + Bluetooth, 66 scene modes, music sync, and works with Alexa/Google. Not individually addressable like Twinkly but at half the price, an excellent choice for most homes. The RGBIC technology means segments can display different colors simultaneously. Popular for Chicago apartment balcony displays.

Best Traditional LED String Lights

Philips LED Smooth Mini Lights ($12/100ct) — The classic. Warm white, energy-efficient, connect up to 5 strands end-to-end. UL-rated for indoor/outdoor use. Weatherproof for Chicago's snow, ice, and sub-zero temperatures. Available at every Target and Home Depot in the Chicago metro. The bulbs stay cool to the touch — safe for trees and mantels.

Home Accents Holiday C9 LED ($25/50ct) — Best for rooflines and outdoor displays. The large C9 bulbs are visible from the street, faceted design creates beautiful light dispersion. Commercial-grade construction handles Chicago winter storms. 80% less energy than incandescent C9s.

Best Outdoor Christmas Lights

Icicle Lights by Brizled ($30/300ct) — Transform your Chicago home's roofline into a winter wonderland. 30-foot length with 8 lighting modes, timer function, and connectable up to 3 sets. IP44 waterproof rating handles Lake Michigan's lake-effect snow. Clear wire option blends with gutters; green wire option disappears into garland.

Star Shower Ultra 9 Projector ($50) — Cover your entire home in holiday patterns without climbing a ladder. Projects 9 different patterns up to 3,200 sq ft. Ideal for Chicago homeowners who don't want to battle ice on roofs. Stake mount with 20-foot cord. Timer function runs 6 hours on, 18 hours off.

Chicago Holiday Light Displays to Visit

  • Magnificent Mile Lights Festival — Over 1 million lights along Michigan Avenue from the Chicago River to Oak Street. Free. Mid-November through mid-January
  • ZooLights at Lincoln Park Zoo — 3 million lights, ice sculptures, train rides. Free admission. November through January
  • Lightscape at Chicago Botanic Garden — Illuminated trail through nature displays. $30-$40 tickets. November through January
  • Brookfield Zoo Holiday Magic — Over 2 million lights, ice carving, light shows. $25-$30. December weekends

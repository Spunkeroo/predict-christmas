Holiday Spending Forecast for 2026

The National Retail Federation projects total holiday spending in 2026 to reach between $960 billion and $990 billion, representing a 3-4% increase over 2025. This growth is driven by a combination of moderate wage growth, stable employment numbers, and continued consumer willingness to spend on experiences and gifts despite inflation concerns.

The per-household breakdown tells a more nuanced story. The average American household is expected to spend approximately $1,050 on holiday gifts in 2026, up from roughly $1,010 in 2025. However, spending patterns vary significantly by income bracket:

The most significant trend is the continued shift toward online shopping. E-commerce is projected to account for 38-40% of total holiday retail sales in 2026, up from approximately 36% in 2025. Mobile commerce specifically is growing fastest, with over 55% of online holiday purchases expected to be made on smartphones.

Economic Factors Shaping the Season

Inflation and pricing. Consumer price inflation has moderated to approximately 2.5-3% in early 2026, down from the peaks of 2022-2023 but still above pre-pandemic levels. Retailers are expected to offer slightly more aggressive discounts than 2025, with average markdowns of 25-35% during peak sale events compared to 22-30% last year.

Consumer credit. Credit card debt reached record levels in late 2025, which may temper spending among middle-income households. Buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) services continue to grow, with an estimated 45% of holiday shoppers expected to use BNPL for at least one purchase in 2026. This is a double-edged sword: it enables spending but creates deferred financial pressure.

Employment. The labor market remains relatively strong with unemployment around 4.1%. Wage growth is running at approximately 3.5% year-over-year. These factors support consumer confidence, though not at the exuberant levels seen in 2021-2022.

Housing costs. Elevated housing costs (rent and mortgage payments) continue to squeeze disposable income for many households. This is the primary reason analysts expect moderate rather than strong holiday spending growth.

When to Get the Best Deals (Month by Month)

Timing matters enormously in holiday shopping. The difference between buying at the right time and the wrong time can mean 30-50% savings on the same item. Here is the deal calendar based on historical patterns and 2026 projections:

October 2026

Best deals on: Amazon Prime Big Deal Days (expected mid-October), electronics early deals, winter clothing. Amazon's fall Prime event has become the unofficial start of the holiday shopping season. In 2025, it offered some of the best prices of the year on Amazon devices, TVs, and headphones. Expect the same pattern in 2026.

November 2026

Best deals on: TVs, laptops, gaming consoles, smart home devices, appliances. The first three weeks of November see escalating deals as retailers compete for early shoppers. The week before Thanksgiving is when many retailers launch their "Black Friday Preview" sales.

Black Friday / Cyber Monday (Nov 27-30)

Best deals on: Everything. This remains the single best window for deals across all categories. TVs historically see their lowest prices of the year on Black Friday. Laptops, headphones, gaming accessories, and smart home devices follow the same pattern.

December 1-15

Best deals on: Clothing, toys (as retailers clear inventory), subscription services, digital gifts. Post-Cyber Monday, deals shift from electronics to other categories. This is a good window for clothing, fashion accessories, and subscription gifts.

December 16-24

Worst time to buy: Most items return to regular pricing. Last-minute shoppers pay a premium. The exceptions are gift cards (always face value) and digital subscriptions (often discounted to capture last-minute buyers).

The Optimal Shopping Strategy

Buy electronics and big-ticket items during Amazon's October Prime event or Black Friday. Buy clothing and fashion in early December. Buy toys in late November when selection is best. Buy gift cards and subscriptions anytime. Start early, finish by December 15, and you will pay less and stress less than 80% of shoppers.

Top Trending Gifts for 2026

Based on search trends, social media buzz, pre-order data, and prediction market signals, these are the gift categories generating the most excitement for Christmas 2026:

1. AI Companion Devices

Standalone AI devices that serve as personal assistants, creative partners, and learning tools. The category has matured significantly from the underwhelming 2024 launches. Devices with screens, cameras, and genuine utility are driving adoption. This is the "it" tech gift category for 2026.

2. Smart Rings and Wearable Health Tech

Smart rings have crossed from niche to mainstream. Samsung Galaxy Ring, Oura Ring Gen 4, and new entrants from Apple and Google have made health-tracking rings the wearable gift of the season. Compact, stylish, and genuinely useful for sleep and fitness tracking.

3. Portable Power Stations

Climate events, outdoor recreation growth, and remote work have made portable power stations one of the fastest-growing gift categories. Brands like Jackery, EcoFlow, and Anker are offering increasingly capable, compact units at accessible prices.

4. E-Bikes and Micro-Mobility

Folding e-bikes and electric scooters continue to grow as practical transportation gifts. Urban commuters especially value them. Prices have dropped significantly, with quality e-bikes now available under $1,000.

5. Craft and DIY Kits for Adults

The experience-over-stuff trend continues. Premium craft kits (pottery, candle-making, leatherworking, fermentation) are popular gifts that provide ongoing engagement rather than one-time novelty.

6. Subscription Experiences

Subscription gifts for streaming, meal kits, book clubs, wine clubs, and educational platforms. These are convenient to gift (no shipping delays), personalized, and provide ongoing value.

How AI Is Changing Holiday Shopping in 2026

AI tools are reshaping how people shop for gifts, and 2026 is the year this impact becomes unmistakable:

AI gift recommendation engines. Retailers like Amazon, Walmart, and Target have deployed AI-powered gift finders that go beyond basic filters. Describe the recipient ("active 30-year-old who loves cooking and hiking") and get personalized recommendations with price ranges. These tools are surprisingly good in 2026.

Price tracking and prediction. AI-powered price tracking tools now predict with reasonable accuracy whether a product's price will drop further or if the current price is the best it will get this season. Camelcamelcamel and similar tools have integrated AI forecasting that helps shoppers decide when to buy.

AI-generated gift guides. While the internet is now flooded with AI-generated gift guides (many of low quality), the best ones use AI to aggregate and analyze thousands of reviews, price histories, and return rates to surface genuinely good recommendations. Look for guides that cite specific data rather than generic descriptions.

Chatbot shopping assistants. Every major retailer now offers an AI chatbot that can help you find products, check availability, track orders, and process returns. These have improved dramatically from the frustrating chatbots of 2023-2024 and now handle most common shopping questions effectively.

Common Christmas Shopping Mistakes to Avoid

  • Impulse buying during flash sales. Not every "deal" is a real deal. Retailers inflate original prices before discounting. Always check price history on CamelCamelCamel or Google Shopping before buying.
  • Ignoring return policies. Holiday return windows vary by retailer. Most extend returns for items bought in November-December through late January. Verify the return policy before buying, especially for electronics and clothing.
  • Overlooking digital gifts. Gift cards, subscriptions, e-books, and online course access are instant, never out of stock, and increasingly appreciated. They are not "lazy gifts" — they are practical gifts that let recipients choose exactly what they want.
  • Buying trending tech without research. The newest product is not always the best value. Last year's model at 40% off often outperforms this year's model at full price for most users.
  • Forgetting shipping costs. A product that costs $5 less from a smaller retailer but charges $8 for shipping is not a deal. Factor in total cost including shipping and potential return shipping.
  • Not setting a budget. Households that set and track a holiday budget spend 20-30% less than those who shop without one, according to consumer finance research. Use a spreadsheet or budgeting app to track spending against your target.

Budget-Friendly Gift Strategies

You do not need to overspend to give great gifts. These strategies consistently produce the best value:

  1. Buy consumables. Premium food items, coffee, tea, candles, and specialty snacks are universally appreciated, reasonably priced, and never need to be returned or stored.
  2. Group gifting. Instead of five people each buying a $50 gift for grandma, pool $250 for one meaningful gift she will actually use.
  3. Shop outlet and refurbished. Apple's refurbished store, Amazon Renewed, and manufacturer outlets offer 20-40% savings on products that are functionally identical to new.
  4. Use cashback and rewards. Stack credit card cashback (5% holiday categories on Chase, Discover), shopping portal cashback (Rakuten, TopCashback), and retailer loyalty points. Combined, these can add 10-15% effective savings.
  5. Give your time and skills. Homemade gifts, photo books, hand-written letters, and experience vouchers (cooking a meal, babysitting, teaching a skill) are free and often more valued than purchased items.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the absolute best time to buy Christmas gifts?
Black Friday and Cyber Monday (November 27-30, 2026) for electronics and big-ticket items. October Prime Day for Amazon devices and select electronics. Early December for clothing and fashion. The overall best strategy is to finish 80% of your shopping between Black Friday and December 10th.

Will prices be higher or lower than last year?
Slightly lower. Moderate inflation, stable supply chains, and competitive retailer pressure are expected to produce slightly better deals than 2025, particularly on electronics and smart home devices.

What is the best gift under $50?
An Amazon Echo Dot on Black Friday ($20-25), premium specialty food/coffee ($25-40), or a streaming service annual subscription ($50). These consistently score highest on recipient satisfaction surveys.

Should I use buy-now-pay-later?
Only if you would buy the item anyway and can make all payments on time. BNPL interest rates and late fees can turn a good deal into an expensive mistake. If you need BNPL to afford gifts, consider reducing your gift budget instead.

Are gift cards still a good gift?
Yes. Consumer surveys consistently show gift cards as one of the most desired gifts. They are not impersonal — they are practical. Choose a retailer or service the recipient actually uses. Avoid prepaid debit cards (they often have fees and expire).
